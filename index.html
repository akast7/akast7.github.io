01 — About

Building intelligent
clinical systems

I am a PhD candidate at the University of Rochester working at the intersection of artificial intelligence, medical imaging, and clinical decision-making.

My research develops deep learning frameworks that integrate multimodal data—CT imaging, EEG, clinical text, and patient history—to solve high-stakes challenges in neurological prognostication. I am particularly interested in how foundational models can be adapted for early disease prediction and clinical decision support.

I am advised by Prof. Axel Wismüller in the AI in Radiology Laboratory, Department of Imaging Sciences. My work has been recognized with the Best Scientific Poster Award at SPIE Medical Imaging.

03 — Research

Selected projects

Featured Project

Multimodal AI for Clinical Prognostication

Developed CLAIR (Contrastive Language and Image Reasoning), a novel multimodal framework that integrates head CT imaging with clinical patient data through a cross-attention mechanism for neurological prognostication.

CLAIR is the first model of its kind to enable accurate neurological prognostication within 24 hours post-cardiac arrest—a significant improvement over the current 72-hour clinical standard. Achieved state-of-the-art AUC-ROC of 0.94.

Currently collaborating with Yale University and UC Irvine to build one of the largest multimodal datasets in this field, integrating CT, MRI, EEG, and EMR data for CLAIR 2.0 development.
